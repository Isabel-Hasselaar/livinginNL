Schools and Education

The Netherlands has a great education system. It has high rates of people with post-graduate degrees. Because of the large international community in NL, there are many international, American, or English speaking schools.

Amsterdam

International School of Amsterdam

ISA is an international day school in Amsterdam. It is English speaking and uses the US IB or international baccalaureate system. It has about 1300 students with over 50 nationalities. It has preschool, primary, and secondary education.

The ASH has an education system that further mimicks the American eductation system. Instead of having primary and secondary school, it has the Elementary, Middle, and High School program. Dutch Education

Of course you could be planning to have your children go to a dutch school. This makes sense if your kids are young and if you are planning to stay for a long time. There Dutch education system is great. It is divided into basisschool and middelbare school. This is similar to primary and secondary school. There are lots of primary schools everywhere in every town. They are small schools, but have lots of children in them. There is usually only one class per grade so kids will have the same peers in their class every year. In secondary education children are divided into three types of schools depending on their academic level. There is VMBO, Havo, and VWO, VMBO would be the lowest level and VWO the highest and most difficult. What level they go to will also determine their possibilities for universities. This system will allow them to spend time learning material that is appropriate for them with kids on their same level.

Universities and Studying in NL

The Netherlands has become a really popular place to study among internationals. If you are a student planning to move to the Netherlands or a parent that has a child that will go to uni then here are some reasons why studying in NL could be a great experience.

  1. A wide range of degrees are taught in English. The Netherlands is one of the first non-English speaking countries to have a university study program in English. There are more than 2,100 courses in English that are available.
  2. A diploma from a dutch university is recognized internationally.
  3. Study costs are affordable. It is a lot cheaper to study in NL than in the UK or USA. It is especially affordable for EU students.
